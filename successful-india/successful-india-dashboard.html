Honesty mode
"Jobless growth" is not a recent invention. Employment elasticity has been falling since the 1991 liberalisation, as growth concentrated in capital-intensive services that don't hire at scale. This government inherited the pattern — what changed was the marketing of the aggregate while the wage line went flat underneath it.

Honesty mode · cuts both ways
In the government's favour: ASER 2024 recorded the biggest improvement in the report's 20-year history, driven by government schools and the NEP 2020 foundational-literacy push. Real, ground-level, worth crediting.

Against it: India's own government learning survey, the NAS, is statistically unreliable even for comparing states (Johnson & Parrado, 2021). When the official machinery grades its own progress, treat it like a student grading their own exam.

The AI frontier: an order of magnitude

Stanford 2025 Global AI Vibrancy score. India is 3rd — and nowhere near.
Honesty mode
India's per-capita "unicorn productivity" is actually low — ~2.5 founders per 100k first-generation immigrants vs Israel's 43 (Stanford VC Initiative). So it's a volume story, not a density story. The point stands: the volume is walking out the door, and India's own sovereign AI compute (~38,000 GPUs, mid-2025) was near-zero until 2024 — against single US clusters of 100,000+ GPUs.

Honesty mode
The government did respond — the ANRF (~$10B over 5 years) and a ₹1 lakh crore RDI fund. Real policy. But the reality check that punctures the press release: of the previous year's RDI allocation, only about 15% was actually spent. The problem isn't only that we don't allocate — it's that we can't deploy what we do. You can't fix a research culture with a fund you don't disburse.

Electoral Bonds

✓ Supreme Court judgment, not opinion

On 15 Feb 2024 a unanimous 5-judge Constitution Bench struck down the scheme as unconstitutional — anonymous corporate funding violated voters' right to information (Art. 19(1)(a)). The court ordered the data disclosed.

~48% of all bond donations → the ruling party; by one ADR analysis, ~90% of corporate bond money. Scheme worth ~₹16,518 cr. The court warned it could enable "quid pro quo."

The Enforcement Asymmetry

✓ Govt data + ⚠ journalistic estimate — kept separate

Government's own Parliament replies: the ED registered 193 cases against politicians in ten years and secured convictions in two. Overall PMLA conviction rate since 2019: under 5% (42 of 911).

Not government data: the Ministry of Finance says it keeps no party-wise breakdown. Separately, an Indian Express investigation and a 14-party SC plea put the opposition share of ED/CBI cases at ~54% (pre-2014) rising to ~95% (post-2014).

An agency that rarely convicts — and, per journalism the government won't confirm or deny, rarely investigates its own side.

The Institutional Scoreboard

✓ Independent global indices

V-Dem reclassified India from liberal democracy (2014) to "electoral autocracy" (by 2020), with a ~20% fall in judicial-autonomy indicators. Freedom House: "Free" → "Partly Free" (2021).

RSF press-freedom rank: 150 → 161 of 180. The legal right to dissent survives on paper; the practical possibility, per the Journal of Democracy, largely does not.

V-Dem 2025 · Freedom House · RSF · Journal of Democracy
Honesty mode · the caveat you specifically asked for
India has suspended civil liberties far more severely before. The Emergency (1975–77) was a formal suspension of democracy — press censored by law, opposition jailed en masse. Sedition law (IPC 124A) has been abused by governments of every stripe since independence. The institutions were never pristine. What the data describes now is not unprecedented in kind — it's a gradual, deniable version of older authoritarian instincts, run through agencies and funding structures rather than a declared Emergency. That makes it worse in its sophistication, not necessarily its severity — and harder to see.

So what is "Successful India™"?

A country that grew its GDP faster than almost anyone and its median wage barely at all. That put nearly every child in school and taught three in four of them to read below their grade. That produced the best engineering and AI talent on earth and exported it to build someone else's frontier. That spent 0.64% of GDP on the future and couldn't spend even that. And that documented the erosion of its own accountability institutions so thoroughly the evidence comes from its own courts and its own Parliament.

None of this says India is failing, or that one party broke a working machine. Much is cultural, much is inherited, much predates 2014 and would outlast any government. The point is narrower: we've become extraordinarily good at producing numbers that look like success, and extraordinarily reluctant to measure the things that actually compound — learning, wages, retained talent, research, and the health of the institutions that keep power honest. GDP was always going to grow. We chose it as the scoreboard precisely because it flatters us.

The children who can't read the grade-2 textbook are eight years old. In 2040, when the demographic window closes, they'll be twenty-two. That is the actual deadline. Everything else is a photograph.
