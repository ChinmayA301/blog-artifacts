# Source notes

## ASER

ASER is a nationwide rural household survey, not a school administrative
dataset. Children are assessed one-on-one in the household, and school type is
recorded. The 2024 national findings report 649,491 children in 17,997 villages
across 605 rural districts.

The study uses Table 4's "all children" Standard III reading measure. This is
deliberate: the often-cited 23.4% value is government-school only. Table 5's
government-plus-private weighted value is 27.1%, while Table 4's all-children
value is 27.0%.

## Directorate of Enforcement

The ED statistics page is a primary government source and was read as a
snapshot dated 31 March 2026. Its published 93.33% conviction percentage is
explicitly 56 conviction outcomes divided by 60 completed trials.

Ratios using prosecution complaints or ECIRs as denominators describe pipeline
throughput, not the legal success rate among completed trials. Cases are not
necessarily drawn from the same cohort.

## FATF

The 2024 mutual evaluation supplies institutional context: investigation,
prosecution, and trial can be separated by long delays; legal challenges and
capacity constraints affected throughput; and a high share among completed
trials can coexist with a small number of concluded cases.

## Electoral bonds

The Election Commission's disclosure is primary raw data. This artifact does
not infer an enforcement relationship from it. A defensible linkage study
would require entity resolution, dates, case-stage histories, a comparison
group, and a pre-specified identification design.

## Verification tracker

`data/source_audit.csv` separates verified, derived, corrected, and
not-verifiable-from-aggregate-data claims. A secondary or journalistic claim is
not upgraded to a primary fact merely because it is widely repeated.
